# Record of Processing Activities (RoPA)
*GDPR Article 30 — Template*

> **Who must maintain a RoPA:** Controllers and processors with ≥250 employees, OR who carry out processing likely to result in risk, process special categories, or process criminal data. In practice, most organizations processing personal data should maintain one.
>
> **LGPD equivalent:** LGPD Art. 37 requires controllers and operators to maintain records of personal data processing operations.

## How to Use This Register

1. Add one row per distinct processing activity.
2. A "processing activity" is a coherent set of operations with the same purpose (e.g., "Customer order processing", "Employee payroll", "Marketing email campaigns").
3. Review and update whenever a new processing activity starts, changes materially, or ends.
4. Make this register available to your supervisory authority on request.

## Appendix: Legal Basis Reference

| GDPR Art. 6(1) | Basis | Key Condition |
|---|---|---|
| (a) | Consent | Freely given, specific, informed, unambiguous |
| (b) | Contract | Necessary to perform or enter into contract with data subject |
| (c) | Legal obligation | EU or member state law requires it |
| (d) | Vital interests | Life or death situation |
| (e) | Public task | Official authority vested by law |
| (f) | Legitimate interests | Balancing test — not overridden by data subject's rights |
