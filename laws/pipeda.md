# PIPEDA — Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act
*S.C. 2000, c. 5 — Canada — Effective 1 January 2004 (full scope)*

> **Note:** PIPEDA is being replaced by **Bill C-27 / CPPA** (Consumer Privacy Protection Act), which was tabled in 2022 and remains under review in Parliament as of 2025. Organisations should monitor its progress. This document covers current PIPEDA requirements.

## Overview

Canada's federal private-sector privacy law. Applies to organisations that collect, use, or disclose personal information in the course of commercial activities, and to personal information of employees of federal works, undertakings, or businesses.

**Enforcement:** Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada (OPC). The OPC can investigate complaints and issue findings but cannot impose fines (under PIPEDA). The proposed CPPA would introduce fines up to $25 million or 5% of global revenue.

**Provincial equivalents:** Quebec (Law 25 / Bill 64 — in force 2022-2023, with GDPR-like elements), Alberta (PIPA), British Columbia (PIPA BC). Provincial laws apply within their provinces if substantially similar to PIPEDA.

---

## Scope (Section 4)

Applies to:
- Private-sector organisations in Canada that **collect, use, or disclose personal information** in the course of **commercial activities**
- Federal works, undertakings, or businesses (interprovincial/international activities): employee personal information also covered

**Exclusions:**
- Government institutions (covered by Privacy Act)
- Non-commercial activities
- Provinces with substantially similar legislation (Quebec, Alberta, BC for private-sector)
- Journalistic, artistic, or literary purposes

---

## The 10 Fair Information Principles (Schedule 1)

PIPEDA is built around 10 principles from the Canadian Standards Association Model Code:

| # | Principle | Key Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Accountability** | Designate an individual responsible for compliance; implement policies |
| 2 | **Identifying purposes** | Identify purposes before or at time of collection |
| 3 | **Consent** | Obtain knowledge and consent (express or implied) |
| 4 | **Limiting collection** | Collect only what is necessary for the stated purposes |
| 5 | **Limiting use, disclosure, and retention** | Use/disclose only for purposes identified; retain only as long as needed |
| 6 | **Accuracy** | Keep information accurate, complete, and up to date |
| 7 | **Safeguards** | Use security appropriate to sensitivity |
| 8 | **Openness** | Make policies and practices readily available |
| 9 | **Individual access** | Upon request, inform of existence, use, and disclosure; allow correction |
| 10 | **Challenging compliance** | Individuals can challenge compliance to the designated individual |

## Consent

PIPEDA relies primarily on **knowledge and consent**. Consent may be:

- **Express** — clear, affirmative act (required for sensitive information)
- **Implied** — reasonably inferred from action or inaction (acceptable for less sensitive information)

### Consent is **not required** when:

- Clearly in the individual's interest and consent cannot be obtained in timely fashion
- Necessary to investigate breach of agreement or Canadian/provincial law
- Emergency situations involving life, health, or security
- Journalistic, literary, or artistic purposes
- Publicly available information (as defined in regulations)
- Statistical, scholarly, or research purposes

### When consent is **invalid:**

- Collected by deception
- Misrepresented purposes
- Conditions for service attached inappropriately

## Individual Rights

| Right | Description |
|---|---|
| **Access** | Right to know what personal information is held, how it's used, to whom disclosed |
| **Correction** | Right to challenge accuracy and completeness; have inaccuracies corrected |
| **Withdrawal of consent** | Right to withdraw consent at any time (with reasonable notice), subject to legal restrictions |
| **Complaint** | Right to complain to the OPC |

**Response time:** No fixed statutory deadline; OPC guidance suggests 30 days.

---

## Breach Notification (Breach of Security Safeguards Regulations — 2018)

Mandatory since 1 November 2018:

- Notify the **OPC** of breaches that pose a **real risk of significant harm** to individuals
- Notify **affected individuals** of breaches posing real risk of significant harm
- Maintain **breach records** for 24 months

**"Significant harm" includes:** bodily harm, humiliation, damage to reputation or relationships, loss of employment, business or professional opportunities, financial loss, identity theft, negative effects on a credit record.

**No fixed deadline** — notification must be made as soon as feasible after determination.

---

## Sensitive Information

PIPEDA does not define special categories explicitly, but requires **higher protection** and generally **express consent** for:
- Medical/health records
- Income information
- Ethnic, racial, or political data
- Religious beliefs
- Sexual behaviour
- Financial information (beyond general credit)

---

## Third-Party Transfers

When transferring personal information to a third party for processing:
- **Remain accountable** — the transferring organisation is still responsible
- Contractual or other means must ensure comparable protection
- Individuals may not need to be informed of all sub-processors, but must be informed that data may be transferred to another jurisdiction

## Quebec — Law 25 (An Act to Modernize Legislative Provisions Respecting the Protection of Personal Information)

Quebec's updated law is the most GDPR-like in Canada and fully in force since September 2023:

| Feature | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Privacy impact assessments | Required before collecting PI, exporting PI, or acquiring a business involving PI |
| Explicit consent | Required for sensitive information |
| Right to erasure | Right to de-indexation and erasure |
| Right to data portability | Right to receive PI in structured format |
| Automated decisions | Right to know, be informed, and have a human review automated decisions |
| Privacy by design | Must integrate privacy at design stage |
| Breach notification | 72 hours to Commission d'accès à l'information (CAI) |
| Penalties | Up to C$25 million or 4% of worldwide turnover |
