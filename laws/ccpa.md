# CCPA/CPRA — California Consumer Privacy Act / California Privacy Rights Act
*California Civil Code §1798.100 et seq. — CCPA effective 1 January 2020; CPRA amendments effective 1 January 2023*

## Overview

The CCPA, as amended by the CPRA, is the most comprehensive US state privacy law. It grants California residents broad rights over their personal information and imposes significant obligations on businesses.

**Enforcement:** California Privacy Protection Agency (CPPA) and California Attorney General.

**Penalties:** Up to $2,500 per unintentional violation, $7,500 per intentional violation. For children's data: $7,500 per violation. No statutory cap per breach event.

---

## Scope — Who Must Comply (§1798.140(d))

A for-profit business that:
1. Does business in California AND
2. Meets **any one** of:
   - Annual gross revenues > **$25 million**
   - Annually buys, sells, or shares personal information of **≥100,000 consumers or households**
   - Derives **≥50% of annual revenues** from selling or sharing consumers' personal information

**Non-profits and government agencies are generally exempt.**

---

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Consumer** | California resident |
| **Personal information** | Information that identifies, relates to, or could reasonably be linked with a consumer or household |
| **Sensitive personal information (SPI)** | Elevated category — see below |
| **Sale** | Disclosing personal information for monetary or other valuable consideration |
| **Sharing** | Disclosing personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising |
| **Business** | For-profit entity meeting threshold above |
| **Service provider** | Processes PI on behalf of a business per written contract |
| **Contractor** | New CPRA category — similar to service provider with additional safeguards |
| **Third party** | Entity that is not the business, service provider, or contractor |

---

## Sensitive Personal Information (SPI) — §1798.140(ae)

Higher protection for:
- **SSN, driver's licence, state ID, passport number**
- **Financial account numbers + credentials**
- **Precise geolocation**
- **Racial or ethnic origin, religious or philosophical beliefs, union membership**
- **Personal communications** (mail, email, texts) — unless sent to the business
- **Genetic data**
- **Biometric data** for unique identification
- **Health information**
- **Sex life or sexual orientation**

Consumers have the right to **limit use and disclosure of SPI** (§1798.121).

## Consumer Rights

| Right | Section | Scope | Response Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Right to know** (what is collected) | §1798.100/110 | Categories + specific pieces; 12-month lookback | 45 days (+45 extension) |
| **Right to know** (what is disclosed) | §1798.115 | Third parties to whom PI was sold/disclosed | 45 days |
| **Right to delete** | §1798.105 | Delete PI; instruct service providers to delete | 45 days |
| **Right to correct** | §1798.106 | Correct inaccurate PI | 45 days |
| **Right to opt-out of sale/sharing** | §1798.120 | "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" link | Immediately |
| **Right to limit SPI use** | §1798.121 | Limit to necessary purposes | Immediately |
| **Right to non-discrimination** | §1798.125 | Cannot deny goods/services or charge different price for exercising rights | — |
| **Right to know about automated decision-making** | §1798.185 | Opt-out of ADM; access to significant decisions made about consumer | Per regulations |

## Business Obligations

### Notices Required

| Notice | When |
|---|---|
| **Privacy notice at collection** | Before or at the time of collection |
| **Privacy policy** | Publicly available; updated annually |
| **"Do Not Sell or Share"** link | Homepage + mobile app if selling/sharing |
| **"Limit Use of SPI"** link | If SPI used beyond necessary purposes |

### Privacy Policy Must Include (§1798.130)

- Categories of PI collected in past 12 months
- Purposes for collection
- Categories of third parties to whom PI is disclosed
- Consumer rights and how to exercise them
- Contact information for submitting requests
- Date of last update

### Data Minimisation (CPRA — §1798.100(a)(3))

Businesses may only collect, use, retain, and share PI that is **reasonably necessary and proportionate** to the stated purpose.

### Contracts with Service Providers/Contractors

Must include:
- Prohibition on selling or sharing PI
- Prohibition on using PI beyond the service
- Deletion or return of PI at end of service
- Audit rights
- Sub-processor flow-down requirements

---

## Deletion Exceptions (§1798.105(d))

Businesses may deny erasure if data is necessary to:
- Complete a transaction / provide a requested service
- Detect security incidents
- Debug errors
- Exercise free speech or another legal right
- Comply with a legal obligation
- Conduct research in the public interest
- Enable internal uses reasonably expected by the consumer
- Comply with a legal obligation

---

## Children's Data

- Under 13: **opt-in consent** from parent required before selling/sharing (§1798.120(c))
- 13–15: consumer must **opt-in** before selling/sharing their PI
- Penalty for knowingly selling PI of consumers under 16: $7,500 per violation

---

## Enforcement and Cure Period

- CPPA can investigate and impose penalties without actual harm.
- **CPRA removed** the 30-day cure period for most violations (effective 1 January 2023).
- Businesses still have 30 days to cure violations involving children's PI.
- **Private right of action** (§1798.150): only for data breaches involving non-encrypted/non-redacted PI — statutory damages $100–$750 per consumer per incident or actual damages.

## Relationship to Other US Laws

| Law | Interaction |
|---|---|
| HIPAA | Health data covered by HIPAA is largely exempt from CCPA |
| GLBA | Financial data regulated by GLBA is exempt |
| FERPA | Student records regulated by FERPA are exempt |
| FCRA | Consumer reporting data regulated by FCRA is exempt |
| Other state laws | Virginia (VCDPA), Colorado (CPA), Texas (TDPSA), etc. — similar structure but different thresholds |
