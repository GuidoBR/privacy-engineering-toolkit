# PDPA — Personal Data Protection Act
*Thailand: B.E. 2562 (2019) — Effective 1 June 2022*
*Singapore: Personal Data Protection Act 2012 (No. 26 of 2012) — Effective 2 July 2014*

> **Two separate laws share the "PDPA" acronym.** This document covers both. They share common principles but differ in structure and enforcement.

---

## Thailand PDPA

### Overview

Thailand's comprehensive personal data protection law, modelled substantially on the GDPR. After multiple deferrals, all provisions have been in force since 1 June 2022.

**Enforcement:** Personal Data Protection Committee (PDPC) and Expert Committee.

**Penalties:**
- Criminal: up to 1 year imprisonment and/or ฿1 million fine (sensitive data violations: up to 1 year / ฿500,000)
- Civil: actual damages + up to double punitive damages
- Administrative: up to ฿5 million

### Scope (Section 4)

Applies to personal data controllers and processors that collect, use, or disclose personal data of **individuals in Thailand**, including foreign organisations.

**Exclusions:** Public administration, media/broadcast for public interest, parliament, courts, credit bureaus (separate law), national security.

### Legal Bases (Section 24)

| Basis | Description |
|---|---|
| **Consent** | Freely given, specific, informed, unambiguous |
| **Contract** | Necessary for a contract with the data subject |
| **Vital interests** | To prevent or suppress danger to life/body/health |
| **Public task** | Necessary for public benefit or official authority |
| **Legitimate interests** | Necessary for legitimate interests, balanced against data subject rights |
| **Legal obligation** | Required by law |

**For sensitive data (Section 26):** Explicit consent or specific exceptions (vital interests, legal claims, public health, research, publicly available data, legal obligation).

### Sensitive Data (Section 26)

- Racial/ethnic origin
- Political opinions
- Religious/philosophical beliefs
- Sexual behaviour
- Criminal records
- Health data
- Disability
- Trade union membership
- Genetic/biometric data
- Any other data affecting the data subject in the same manner (PDPC determination)

### Data Subject Rights (Sections 30–43)

| Right | Description |
|---|---|
| Access | Right to access and obtain a copy |
| Rectification | Correct inaccurate, incomplete or misleading data |
| Erasure | Delete or anonymise data when no longer necessary |
| Restriction | Suspend use of personal data |
| Portability | Receive data in commonly used machine-readable format |
| Object | Object to collection, use, or disclosure |
| Non-automated decisions | Object to solely automated decisions |

**Response time:** 30 days.

### Controller Obligations

- Maintain records of processing activities
- Appoint DPO if: large-scale processing, systematic monitoring, or sensitive data processing
- Conduct DPIA for high-risk processing
- Report breaches to PDPC within **72 hours**; notify data subjects if likely to cause harm

### International Transfers (Section 28)

Transfers only to countries with adequate protection (as determined by the PDPC). Where destination lacks adequacy, appropriate safeguards required (contracts, BCRs, etc.).

## Singapore PDPA

### Overview

Singapore's baseline private-sector data protection law. Amended significantly by the Personal Data Protection (Amendment) Act 2020 (effective 1 February 2021), introducing mandatory breach notification, data portability, and higher penalties.

**Enforcement:** Personal Data Protection Commission (PDPC).

**Penalties (post-2021):**
- Up to S$1 million per breach (previous limit)
- From 1 October 2022: up to S$1 million **or** 10% of annual turnover in Singapore (whichever is higher) for organisations with annual turnover > S$10 million

### Scope

Applies to all organisations (private sector) that collect, use, or disclose personal data of individuals in Singapore, including foreign organisations processing data about Singapore residents.

**Exclusions:** Individuals acting in personal/domestic capacity; employees in employment context (to limited extent); public agencies.

### Data Protection Obligations

The PDPA sets out 11 main obligations:

| Obligation | Description |
|---|---|
| **Consent** | Obtain consent; allow withdrawal |
| **Purpose limitation** | Use data only for purposes consented to or as permitted |
| **Notification** | Notify individuals of purposes before or at collection |
| **Access and correction** | Respond to access/correction requests within 30 days |
| **Accuracy** | Take reasonable steps to ensure data is accurate and complete |
| **Protection** | Implement reasonable security arrangements |
| **Retention limitation** | Cease retention when no longer necessary |
| **Transfer limitation** | Transfer only to countries with comparable protection |
| **Data breach notification** | Notify PDPC within 3 business days; notify affected individuals if significant harm |
| **Data portability** | Transfer data at individual's request to another organisation (business-to-business portability) |
| **Do not call registry** | Do not send marketing messages to numbers on the Do Not Call Registry |

### Exceptions to Consent

Similar to GDPR legitimate interests: "legitimate interests" and "business improvement" exceptions allow processing without consent in certain circumstances (added in 2020 amendments).

### Deemed Consent

Consent is deemed in certain scenarios where individual voluntarily provided data for the purpose or the purpose is reasonably necessary for a contract.

### Breach Notification (Mandatory Breach Notification — from 1 February 2021)

- Notify PDPC within **3 calendar days** of becoming aware (if breach is or is likely to be of significant scale or cause significant harm)
- Notify affected individuals without undue delay
- Significant scale: affects ≥500 individuals
- Significant harm: financial loss, physical harm, humiliation, damage to reputation, etc.

### International Transfers

Data may be transferred to a foreign country only if the recipient is bound to a standard of protection comparable to the PDPA (via contractual, binding corporate rules, or approved certification schemes).
