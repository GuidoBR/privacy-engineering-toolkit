# POPIA — Protection of Personal Information Act
*Act 4 of 2013 — South Africa — Effective 1 July 2021 (enforcement)*

## Overview

South Africa's comprehensive data protection law, aligned closely with international standards including the GDPR. Processing of personal information is regulated through eight conditions for lawful processing.

**Enforcement:** Information Regulator (IR) — independent body established under POPIA.

**Penalties:**
- Administrative fines up to **ZAR 10 million** (≈ US$550,000)
- Criminal penalties: imprisonment up to **10 years** (for serious offences), fines, or both
- Civil damages: data subjects may claim damages

---

## Scope (Section 3)

POPIA applies to the **processing of personal information** by a **responsible party** (controller) that is **domiciled in South Africa**, or that is not domiciled in South Africa but **uses automated or non-automated means** in South Africa.

**Exclusions:**
- Processing for purely personal/household purposes
- Processing by public bodies for specific security/criminal purposes
- Processing for journalistic, literary, or artistic purposes (limited)
- Processing that has been de-identified

---

## Key Definitions (Section 1)

| Term | POPIA Equivalent | Definition |
|---|---|---|
| Personal information | Personal information | Information relating to an identifiable, living natural person or juristic person (company) |
| Controller | **Responsible party** | Public or private body that determines the purpose and means of processing |
| Processor | **Operator** | Person processing personal information for a responsible party under authorisation |
| Data subject | Data subject | Person to whom personal information relates |
| Consent | Consent | Voluntary, specific, and informed expression of will |
| Special personal information | Special personal information | See below |

> **Note:** POPIA also protects **juristic persons** (companies), not just natural persons — unlike GDPR.

---

## Eight Conditions for Lawful Processing (Chapter 3)

| # | Condition | Key Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Accountability** | Responsible party must ensure conditions are complied with |
| 2 | **Processing limitation** | Lawfully processed; with consent or another ground |
| 3 | **Purpose specification** | Collected for specific, explicitly defined, lawful purposes |
| 4 | **Further processing limitation** | Compatible with original purpose |
| 5 | **Information quality** | Reasonably correct, complete and up to date |
| 6 | **Openness** | Data subjects must be notified; privacy notice required |
| 7 | **Security safeguards** | Appropriate technical and organisational measures |
| 8 | **Data subject participation** | Access, correction, and deletion rights |

---

## Grounds for Lawful Processing (Section 11)

Processing is lawful when:
- **Consent** of the data subject
- Necessary for a **contract** with the data subject
- **Compliance with an obligation** imposed by law
- **Protects a legitimate interest** of the data subject
- Necessary for the **proper performance of a public law duty**
- Pursuit of **legitimate interests** of the responsible party or third party (not overriding data subject interests)

---

## Special Personal Information (Section 26)

Processing **prohibited** unless an exception applies:

- Religious or philosophical beliefs
- Race or ethnic origin
- Trade union membership
- Political persuasion
- **Health or sex life**
- **Biometric information**
- Criminal behaviour (offences, alleged offences, proceedings, disposals)

**Exceptions (Section 27):**
- Explicit consent
- Statute or right/obligation under international public law
- Race/ethnic origin processing for equity/anti-discrimination purposes
- Legitimate activities of non-profits (members/former members only)
- Data subject made information public
- Legal proceedings / legal advice
- Medical treatment / social services when consent cannot be obtained

---

## Data Subject Rights (Sections 23–25)

| Right | Description | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|
| **Access** | Request confirmation of, and access to, personal information held | 30 days (Section 23) |
| **Correction or deletion** | Request correction of inaccurate, irrelevant, excessive, out of date, misleading, or unlawfully obtained information | 30 days |
| **Object** | Object to processing at any time on reasonable grounds; absolute right to object to direct marketing | 30 days to notify of non-compliance |
| **Complaint to Information Regulator** | Lodge a complaint if not satisfied with outcome | — |

## Information Officer (IO)

**All organisations** must designate an Information Officer (similar to DPO). If no specific person is designated, the head of the private body is automatically the IO.

**Responsibilities:**
- Ensure POPIA compliance
- Deal with requests from data subjects
- Liaise with Information Regulator
- Develop internal compliance framework

---

## Breach Notification (Section 22)

When a responsible party has **reasonable grounds** to believe that personal information has been accessed or acquired by an unauthorised person:

1. Notify the **Information Regulator**
2. Notify the **affected data subjects**

**"As soon as reasonably possible"** — no fixed 72-hour clock like GDPR.

Notification may be delayed if law enforcement investigation would be compromised.

---

## International Transfers (Section 72)

Personal information may only be transferred to a foreign country if:
- The recipient is **subject to a law, binding corporate rules, or binding agreement** that upholds principles substantially similar to POPIA's conditions, **or**
- The **data subject consents** to the transfer, **or**
- The transfer is **necessary for a contract** with the data subject or in their interest, **or**
- The transfer is **necessary for a contract** between the responsible party and a third party in the data subject's interest, **or**
- The transfer is for the **benefit of the data subject** and consent cannot be obtained but if it could be, consent would likely be given
